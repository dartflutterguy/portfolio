Interactive Creative Showcase

ADNEST

Designed an interactive digital showroom that transformed passive viewing into an engaging, persuasive experience. The redesign increased client engagement and improved sales conversion by 25%.

Role

  • User Research
  • Information Architecture

Team

Industry

Timeline

  • 4 Months
01 - THE CHALLENGE

Adnest required a digital showroom capable of doing more than displaying ads. The existing experience lacked interactivity and failed to sustain client engagement during presentations.

Prospective clients passively viewed creative assets without immersion, context, or guided exploration. This reduced emotional impact and limited conversion potential.

The objective was clear:

Design an interactive showroom that transforms passive viewing into an engaging, persuasive experience that drives measurable business outcomes.

02 - EXPERIENCE STRATEGY

To increase engagement and conversion, the showroom was redesigned around three core principles:

  • Guided exploration instead of static browsing
  • Visual storytelling to frame each creative asset
  • Interaction cues that sustain attention and reduce friction

The goal was not just aesthetics, but intentional experience design that leads clients from curiosity to conviction.

03 - INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

The structure of the showroom was reorganized to improve clarity and flow.

Creative assets were grouped by strategic themes, enabling clients to understand campaign intent rather than viewing ads in isolation.

Navigation was simplified to reduce cognitive load and allow seamless transitions between creatives.

This ensured the experience felt curated rather than overwhelming.

04 - INTERACTION & INTERFACE DESIGN

Interactive Ad Showroom

The showroom was designed to make ad exploration intuitive and hands-on, allowing clients to interact with creatives instead of passively viewing static previews.

  • Enabled direct interaction with live ad creatives
  • Reduced friction during sales walkthroughs
  • Contributed directly to a 25% increase in sales

Desktop Creative Preview

The desktop preview demonstrated how creatives would appear within live campaign placements, giving stakeholders clarity on layout, hierarchy, and visual impact before launch.

  • Simulated real campaign viewing conditions
  • Improved confidence during client presentations
  • Reduced ambiguity around placement behavior

Mobile Creative Preview

The mobile preview showcased how the same creative assets would render in mobile environments, ensuring accurate representation across device contexts.

  • Highlighted device-specific behavior
  • Ensured consistency across placements
  • Helped clients evaluate cross-device visibility

Cube Ad Format Integration

A high-impact cube ad format was integrated into the showroom, allowing users to rotate and interact with multiple faces of a 3D ad unit, each presenting distinct visuals or calls-to-action.

  • Enabled multi-surface brand storytelling
  • Increased engagement through interactive motion
  • Expanded creative possibilities beyond static formats
05 - BUSINESS IMPACT

Following the launch of the redesigned showroom:

  • Client engagement during presentations increased significantly
  • Sales conversion improved by 25%
  • The showroom became a strategic sales asset rather than a static gallery

The measurable uplift confirmed that intentional interaction design directly influenced purchasing decisions.

06 - REFLECTION

This project reinforced the importance of designing for persuasion, not just presentation.

By aligning interaction design with business objectives, the showroom evolved into a high-performing conversion tool that supported both creative storytelling and revenue growth.
