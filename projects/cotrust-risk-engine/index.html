01 - CONTEXT

Access to credit remains one of the biggest barriers for micro-businesses across Africa and emerging markets. Financial institutions typically rely on formal financial history to assess loan eligibility, leaving millions of small traders without a reliable way to prove their creditworthiness.

As a result, many micro-entrepreneurs turn to informal lending networks that provide faster access to funds but lack structure, transparency, and long-term financial mobility.

The result is a fragmented lending ecosystem where access to credit is inconsistent and difficult to scale.

02 - THE CREDIT GAP

Ineffective financial data and incomplete credit records contribute to an estimated $236.18B in lost lending opportunities annually across Africa and emerging markets. (source: worldbank 2025)

Many micro-traders operate entirely within informal economies, meaning their financial activity is rarely captured by traditional credit systems.

Without verifiable signals such as transaction history, collateral, or formal income records:

  • lenders struggle to assess risk
  • traders struggle to demonstrate credibility
  • access to capital remains limited

Despite this, these traders operate inside dense local business ecosystems, where trust, proximity, and daily economic activity provide valuable signals that traditional financial models fail to capture.

03 - CREDIT MOBILITY ENGINE

To address this gap, Cotrust began developing the Credit Mobility Engine - a system designed to transform informal business activity into structured credit intelligence.

The engine uses KYC data and geo-location signals to group micro-traders and small businesses into dynamic economic clusters.

Within these clusters, traders gain access to shared financial visibility and portable credit insights that allow lenders to make better risk assessments.

By analyzing these clusters, the platform can generate credit profiles that reflect real economic activity, allowing traders to access funding even without traditional financial records.

This approach enables a new model of lending where credit mobility is driven by economic behavior rather than historical banking data.

04 - PRODUCT INTERFACE

While the Credit Mobility Engine focuses on data and clustering intelligence, the product interface translates this system into an accessible operational experience for users.

The platform provides a centralized workspace where users can monitor credit activity, track loan performance, and manage lending within their cluster ecosystem.

Cluster Credit Dashboard

The dashboard provides an overview of cluster activity, funding availability, and credit insights. It enables users to quickly understand the financial health of their cluster while monitoring ongoing lending activity.

Loan Management Workspace

A dedicated interface for reviewing loan activity, tracking repayments, and managing funding requests within a cluster.

This workspace allows users to move from high-level financial insights into detailed lending operations.

Loan Navigation

A simple navigation flow connecting the dashboard to the loan management workspace, allowing users to transition from cluster-level insights to detailed loan monitoring.

05 - PRODUCT IMPACT

The Credit Mobility Engine introduces a new model for evaluating credit in informal economies.

By combining identity verification, location intelligence, and behavioral signals, the system enables financial institutions to assess lending risk beyond traditional credit history.

Once deployed, the system aims to:

  • expand credit access for underserved traders
  • improve lender confidence through behavioral risk signals
  • enable portable credit profiles across markets
  • digitize informal business ecosystems

By transforming local trading networks into structured financial signals, Cotrust aims to unlock new pathways to capital for millions of micro-businesses.
